BSides Austin 2020 rescheduled

Why reschedule yet again?

For so many of us, 2020 has become synonymous with unthinkable realities and the loss of people, connection, options, and opportunities. It has been and continues to be a tough year.

When we made the decision in early March to postpone our April event until early December, we had high hopes that we would all be able to come together in a safer, post-health crisis environment. Alas, it is not to be. The Austin “Stay Home, Mask, and Otherwise Be Safe” orders are in place through December 15, 2020. In addition to hygiene guidelines, the orders state that social gatherings in groups of 10+ individuals outside of the same household are prohibited.

Of course, that leaves us out.

Many security conferences have shifted their format to online only because of these restrictions. We have attended quite a few of these, and we congratulate the organizers for successfully providing their attendees and sponsors with quality content and experiences.

After spending considerable time evaluating the virtual option for BSides Austin in December versus postponing a second time for an in-person event, we have decided our preference is for a personal experience. We believe that for the kind of vibrant local security community that Austin has, being together is at least as important as sharing fabulous presentations.Therefore, we will forego BSides Austin 2020 in favor of starting to plan BSides Austin 2021.

Our new conference dates are Thursday and Friday, October 7-8, 2021, and we will be back at the Commons Learning Center on the J.J. Pickle Research Campus on Burnet Road. We will hold a new call for papers next spring so that we can offer an updated schedule of fresh talks.

We chose dates late in the year to give us the greatest chance of success. But we are committed to these new dates, come hell or high water. If COVID-19 is still uncontrolled next fall, we will pivot and offer our conference as a virtual event.

We admit that we aren’t happy to be cancelling the 2020 event, but we believe it’s the right approach for this crazy year we’re all enduring. We appreciate your understanding and your support. Know that we miss you all and wish you continued safety. We hope to see you in 2021!
